What is this website?

Welcome to My Top Studio Ghibli! This website showcases my personal top 5 rankings across all things Ghibli. From the most enchanting movies and memorable soundtracks to my favorite characters and foods featured in the films. Each list reflects my own experiences and appreciation for the artistry, emotion, and imagination that make Studio Ghibli so timeless. Whether you’re here to compare favorites or discover something new, I hope this collection celebrates the magic that continues to inspire fans around the world.

Top 5 Studio Ghibli Movies






#5 The Wind Rises
Starting off my list, The Wind Rises stands out for its mature tone and poetic storytelling. Its mix of history, dreams, and melancholy beauty reflects the passion and sacrifice behind creation itself. The film’s quiet reflection on art, love, and impermanence makes it a fitting and touching final masterpiece for Hayao Miyazaki’s career.

#4 Grave of the Fireflies
Though it’s one of the most heartbreaking films ever made, Grave of the Fireflies deserves to be high on the list for its emotional honesty. It captures the human cost of war in a way that’s tender and devastating, reminding me of the strength and love that persist even in tragedy. It’s not an easy watch, but it’s one of Ghibli’s most important works.







#3 Howl's Moving Castle
Howl’s Moving Castle earns its spot for its magical worldbuilding and whimsical romance. The castle’s design, the moving landscapes, and Howl’s mysterious charm make it a constant visual and emotional delight. I love how Sophie’s transformation becomes a metaphor for inner strength and self-acceptance; it’s a story that feels empowering every time I watch it.

#2 My Neighbor Totoro
This film holds a special place in my heart for its pure sense of wonder and simplicity. My Neighbor Totoro reminds me of my childhood and why Ghibli is so beloved. Whether its warmth, innocence, and gentle pacing feel like a comforting childhood memory. Totoro himself is such an iconic, joyful spirit that it’s impossible not to smile every time he appears




#1 Princess Mononoke
Princess Mononoke takes the top spot for its breathtaking storytelling and powerful environmental message. The balance between nature and human ambition, paired with stunning visuals and complex characters like Ashitaka and San, makes it unforgettable. Its emotional depth and epic scope capture everything I love about Studio Ghibli’s ability to blend beauty, chaos, and meaning.

Top 5 Studio Ghibli Soundtracks






#5 Ponyo
• Composer: Joe Hisaishi
Ponyo’s soundtrack bursts with energy and joy, mirroring the film’s childlike wonder and oceanic chaos. Hisaishi’s playful rhythms and grand orchestral flourishes give the movie an adventurous spirit. The iconic theme song, “Ponyo on the Cliff by the Sea,” is irresistibly catchy and full of heart, making it the perfect way to close out this list.

#4 Nausicaä
• Composer: Joe Hisaishi
Nausicaä of the Valley of the Wind has a distinctive, almost futuristic sound that sets it apart from later Ghibli works. Hisaishi combines electronic tones with sweeping orchestral themes, creating a sense of wonder and scale that perfectly matches Nausicaä’s post-apocalyptic yet hopeful world.







#3 Arrietty
• Composer: Cécile Corbel
The Arrietty soundtrack stands out for its unique Celtic-inspired sound, composed by French musician Cécile Corbel. Her harp-driven melodies bring a delicate, airy charm to the film’s miniature world, making everyday objects feel magical. The blend of folk and fantasy creates a refreshing musical tone that feels both grounded and whimsical.

#2 My Neighbor Totoro
• Composer: Joe Hisaishi
Few soundtracks feel as warm and nostalgic as My Neighbor Totoro’s. Hisaishi’s playful melodies, like “The Path of the Wind,” embody the innocence and curiosity of childhood exploration. The music is simple yet deeply emotional, making it one of the most comforting and instantly recognizable scores in Ghibli’s history.




#1 Howl’s Moving Castle
• Composer: Joe Hisaishi
he Howl’s Moving Castle soundtrack sits at the top for its sweeping orchestral beauty and emotional depth. Joe Hisaishi’s compositions perfectly capture the wonder and mystery of Howl’s world, from the whimsical “Merry-Go-Round of Life” to the film’s quieter, more reflective moments. Every note feels magical, romantic, and alive—just like the moving castle itself.

Top 5 Studio Ghibli Characters





#5 Jirou
Jirou is a beautifully nuanced character, who is a dreamer and engineer driven by his love for aviation. His story is one of inspiration and sacrifice, where the pursuit of creation collides with the realities of war. Jirou’s quiet resolve and poetic outlook give The Wind Rises its reflective, bittersweet tone.



#4 San
San is one of Ghibli’s most powerful and complex characters. Fierce, loyal, and deeply connected to the natural world, she embodies the film’s core struggle between humanity and nature. Her inner conflict and courage make her unforgettable; a warrior raised by wolves who fights with passion and purpose.








#3 Jiji
Jiji’s witty, sarcastic personality makes him a standout companion in Kiki’s Delivery Service. Beneath his humor, though, is genuine care for Kiki and her journey toward independence. Jiji adds both levity and warmth to the story, serving as the perfect balance between comic relief and emotional support.

#2 Ponyo
Ponyo captures the purest form of wonder and curiosity that defines Studio Ghibli. Her playful spirit, determination, and love for the human world make her irresistibly endearing. She’s a perfect symbol of freedom and joy, reminding us of childhood’s boundless imagination and the magic that comes from following one’s heart.










#1 Totoro
Totoro is the heart and soul of Ghibli’s magic. Gentle, mysterious, and endlessly comforting, he represents the kindness and wonder of nature itself. His presence transforms ordinary life into something extraordinary, making him not only an icon of the studio but also a timeless symbol of joy and peace.

Favorite Studio Ghibli Foods



Ramen

Seen in Ponyo when Sōsuke’s mom makes a cozy bowl of ramen with ham and egg.

Onigiri

From My Neighbor Totoro, when Satsuki packs lunch for Mei and herself.

Bacon and Eggs

The sizzling, heartwarming breakfast Sophie cooks for Howl and Calcifer in Howl’s Moving Castle.

Tempura and Bento Lunches

Seen in From Up on Poppy Hill and Only Yesterday, showing everyday Japanese life.

Fish and Ham Sandwiches

Ponyo’s favorite comfort food, a mix of land and sea flavors.

Stew and Bread

From Castle in the Sky, a hearty meal shared between Pazu and Sheeta during their adventure.
